FOOD TECHNOLOGY

A Diploma in Food Technology is a specialized program designed to equip students with the necessary skills and knowledge to work in the food processing and production industry. This course provides a deep understanding of food science, processing techniques, and quality control measures that are essential for ensuring the safety, nutritional value, and quality of food products. The duration of the diploma program typically spans 2 to 3 years, combining theoretical coursework with practical training to prepare students for a wide range of careers in the food industry.
The curriculum of a Diploma in Food Technology covers various aspects of food production, preservation, and safety. Students are introduced to the fundamental principles of food science, which includes studying the composition of different food materials, the chemical and biological processes involved in food preservation, and the methods used to process and package food. The primary goal of the program is to prepare students to work in food production facilities, laboratories, and research institutions, where they can apply their knowledge to improve food quality and ensure compliance with safety standards.
One of the key areas of focus in food technology is food processing. Students learn about various methods used to preserve food, such as canning, freezing, drying, fermentation, and pasteurization. They also study the technologies behind modern food processing systems, which help to improve the shelf life and safety of food products. The course also covers packaging techniques, where students gain knowledge about packaging materials, design, and the importance of packaging in preserving food quality and preventing contamination.
